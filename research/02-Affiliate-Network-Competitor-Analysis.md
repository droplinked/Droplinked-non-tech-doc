# Feature Competitor Analysis: Affiliate Network

## Executive Summary

This document analyzes how major e-commerce platforms and affiliate networks handle affiliate programs, commission structures, and marketplace functionality.

---

## Competitor Analysis

### 1. Shopify Collabs

**How They Handle Affiliate Network:**
- **Model**: Two-sided marketplace connecting brands and creators
- **Role Types**: Brand (merchant) and Creator (affiliate)
- **Commission**: Brands set their own rates (typically 5-30%)
- **Application**: Creators apply to brands; brands invite approved creators

**Key Features:**
- Discovery platform for creators to find brands
- Unique tracking links and discount codes
- Automated commission calculation and payment
- Performance dashboard for both parties
- Integration with Shopify admin

**Commission Structure:**
- Percentage of sale (configurable by brand)
- Fixed amount per sale option
- Tiered commissions based on performance
- Cookie duration: Set by brand (30-90 days typical)

**BACs:**
1. Brands control commission rates and terms
2. Creators can apply to multiple brands
3. Automatic tracking via unique links
4. Monthly payouts to creators
5. Minimum payout threshold ($25)

**Strengths:**
- Native Shopify integration
- Automated tracking and payments
- Discovery platform built-in
- No exclusive roles (creators can work with multiple brands)

**Weaknesses:**
- No exclusive co-seller relationship
- Limited to Shopify ecosystem
- Creator must have Shopify Collabs account

### 2. Amazon Associates

**How They Handle Affiliate Network:**
- **Model**: Open affiliate program for Amazon products
- **Role Types**: Affiliate (promoter) and Amazon (merchant)
- **Commission**: Fixed percentage by category (1-10%)
- **Application**: Open application with approval process

**Key Features:**
- Massive product catalog
- Text links, banners, native ads
- 24-hour cookie duration
- SiteStripe toolbar for easy link creation
- Performance reporting dashboard

**Commission Structure:**
- Category-based fixed rates
- Volume-based bonuses for high performers
- No negotiation on rates

**BACs:**
1. Commission rates fixed by Amazon
2. 24-hour cookie window
3. Payment after 60 days (accounting for returns)
4. $10 minimum payout threshold
5. 180-day account activity requirement

**Strengths:**
- Trusted brand increases conversion
- Huge product selection
- Reliable payments
- Easy link creation tools

**Weaknesses:**
- Very low cookie duration (24 hours)
- Fixed rates with no negotiation
- Low commissions on many categories (1-4%)
- Strict program policies

---

### 3. ShareASale

**How They Handle Affiliate Network:**
- **Model**: Third-party affiliate network platform
- **Role Types**: Merchant (advertiser) and Affiliate (publisher)
- **Commission**: Merchant-defined (typically 5-50%)
- **Application**: Affiliates apply to specific merchant programs

**Key Features:**
- Large network of merchants across industries
- Real-time tracking and reporting
- Recurring commission support
- Two-tier affiliate programs
- Coupon code tracking

**Commission Structure:**
- Pay-per-sale (percentage or fixed)
- Pay-per-lead
- Pay-per-click (rare)
- Recurring commissions for subscriptions
- Cookie duration: Merchant-defined (30-120 days)

**BACs:**
1. Merchants set commission terms
2. Affiliates can join multiple merchant programs
3. Real-time click and sale tracking
4. Monthly payments (20th of month)
5. $50 minimum payout threshold

**Strengths:**
- Established network with many merchants
- Robust tracking technology
- Reliable payment processing
- Recurring commission support

**Weaknesses:**
- Network fees reduce effective commission
- Competitive approval process for affiliates
- Interface can be complex for beginners

### 4. Refersion (Shopify App)

**How They Handle Affiliate Network:**
- **Model**: Affiliate marketing platform for e-commerce
- **Role Types**: Merchant and Affiliate
- **Commission**: Fully customizable by merchant
- **Application**: Affiliates receive unique registration links

**Key Features:**
- Customizable affiliate portal
- Automated commission tracking
- Multiple commission structures (percentage, fixed, tiered)
- Custom cookie duration
- PayPal mass payments

**Commission Structure:**
- Percentage of sale (1-100%)
- Fixed amount per sale
- Tiered commissions (performance-based increases)
- Product-specific commissions
- Coupon code commissions

**BACs:**
1. Merchants control all commission settings
2. Customizable cookie duration
3. Automatic commission approval or manual review
4. PayPal or manual payment options
5. No minimum payout (merchant-configurable)

**Strengths:**
- Highly customizable
- White-label affiliate portal
- Automated workflows
- Good for Shopify stores

**Weaknesses:**
- Monthly subscription cost
- Requires separate setup from Shopify
- Learning curve for advanced features

## Best Practices Summary

### 1. **Flexible Commission Structure**
- Allow merchants to set custom rates (5-50% typical range)
- Support percentage, fixed amount, and tiered models
- Product-specific commission overrides

### 2. **Clear Role Definition**
- Distinct roles for product owners (Affiliator) and sellers (Co-seller)
- Clear value proposition for each role
- Easy onboarding process

### 3. **Reliable Tracking**
- Unique tracking links per affiliate
- Cookie-based attribution (30-90 days)
- Coupon code tracking as backup
- Cross-device tracking capability

### 4. **Transparent Reporting**
- Real-time dashboard for both parties
- Click, conversion, and commission tracking
- Payment history and pending amounts
- Performance analytics

### 5. **Automated Payments**
- Automated commission calculation
- Scheduled payout cycles
- Multiple payout methods (PayPal, bank transfer, platform credit)
- Minimum payout thresholds to reduce fees

### 6. **Discovery Platform**
- Marketplace for affiliates to find products
- Search and filter capabilities
- Merchant profiles and product catalogs
- Rating/review system

## Droplinked Recommendations

### Immediate Implementation

1. **Exclusive Role System** (Droplinked Innovation)
   - Affiliator OR Co-seller (not both)
   - Permanent role assignment
   - Clear commitment from both parties
   - *Why*: Prevents conflicts of interest, ensures commitment

2. **Fixed + Manual Commission Modes** (like Refersion)
   - Fixed: One rate for all products
   - Manual: Per-product custom rates
   - Range: 1-99%

3. **Credit-Based Settlement** (Droplinked Innovation)
   - Commissions as shop credits, not direct payments
   - Merchants can use credits for platform fees or withdraw
   - Simplifies tax and compliance

4. **Marketplace Discovery** (like ShareASale)
   - Browse products by category
   - Filter by commission rate, category, merchant
   - Product detail pages with commission info

### Future Enhancements

1. **Tiered Commissions** (like Refersion)
   - Performance-based rate increases
   - Volume bonuses
   - Loyalty rewards

2. **Multi-Currency Support** (Future)
   - Current: USD only (simplified)
   - Future: Multi-currency with real-time conversion

3. **Advanced Tracking**
   - Coupon code tracking
   - Multi-touch attribution
   - Cross-device tracking

## Feature Conflicts Considerations

### Conflict with Shop Currency
- **Issue**: Affiliate requires currency alignment
- **Competitor Approach**: Most platforms use single base currency or handle conversion
- **Droplinked Solution**: Enforce USD-only for affiliate transactions (Phase 1)

### Conflict with On-Chain Recording
- **Issue**: Recorded products become read-only
- **Droplinked Solution**: Separate commission metadata from product data

### Conflict with Payment Split
- **Issue**: Multiple recipients complicate payment flow
- **Droplinked Solution**: Platform holds funds, distributes credits

---

## Conclusion

**Winner**: Refersion for customization + ShareASale for marketplace discovery

**Droplinked Differentiation**: Exclusive roles and credit-based settlement create unique value proposition

**Key Success Factors**: Clear role separation, transparent reporting, reliable tracking, and fair commission structure
